Abstract

We propose general flow as universal affordance for scalable robot manipulation learning in the real world. While previous representation is either embodiment/simulation-dependent or lack fine-grained geometric and semantic to provide details guidance, general flow provide foundation ability and property even to achieve zero-shot human-to-robot skill transfer. In this paper, we define general flow formally, and extract it from both real world 3D human datasets and original RGBD video. New algorithm is design to enhance scale-awareness for general flow prediction, and multiple key properties (e.g. robustness to label noise, common sense acquisition) emergent with training in scale. In addition, with a close-loop controller and a simple heuristic policy derived from general flow prediction, we adopt our methods on a Franka-Emika robot and achieved zero-shot skill transfer with 81\% success rate for all categories of object with totally 18 tasks. All of these shows great potentiality of general flow prediction as foundation affordance for robot learning in the future. We will release our tool, code and weight to benefit the subsequent research.

General Flow



We extend previous work to propose general flow as foundation affordance. Property and downstream application are analysis to reveal its foundational status. We extract general flow label directly from human RGBD video datasets and design new algorithm for general flow prediction. Zero-shot cross-embodiment skill transfer is achieved in the real world. All of these show the great power and potentiality of general flow affordance.

Emergent Properties of General Flow

With general flow prediction task trained in scale, our model acquire multiple emergent properties, which is impossible for small-scale imitation learning setting.

  • Semantic Rich & Controllable [(a)(b)]: semantic of flow could be changed easily by switching language instruction.

  • Robust to the Label Noise [(c)(d)]: even with severe noise in label (significant deviation in 'open Safe' and almost static in 'pickup Toy Car'), our model could predict right trend. (red indicates label and green indicates prediction in the figure.)

  • Spatial Commonsense Acquisition [(e)(f)]: the model adjust its prediction scale to fit for object spatial relationship (both end on the table, and larger scale for longer distance).
